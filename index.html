What is the fixed charge coverage ratio?
the fixed charge coverage ratio (FCCR) is a measurement of a firm's capacity to meet fixed charge obligations such as lease expenses and interest expenses. Higher fixed charge coverage ratio indicates a financially healthy and less risky company. Lower fixed charge coverage ratio indicates a company that is struggling to meet it's payments.
How to calculate fixed charge coverage ratio?
To calculate fixed charge coverage ratio you should use the following formula:
fixed charge coverage ratio = (EBITDA - capex - cash taxes) / (interest + principal + capital lease payments)
example: company x has a monthly expenses include lease payments of $7,000. The company generated EBITDA of $680,000 and $43,000 of annual principal repayments. Annually it spends $3,000 to replace some equipments in the current year the company paid $52,000 in taxes, calculate the fixed charge coverage ratio of the company?

using the fixed charge coverage ratio the answer will be:

Why use fixed charge coverage ratio?
The fixed charge coverage ratio is usually used by lenders analyzing the amount of cash flow a firm has available for debt repayment. A low ratio indicates a lack of ability to make payments on fixed charges.
